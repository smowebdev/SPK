Our Role

Our Role in Government

How Stanford Knight & Partners Helped Build Vanuatu’s Citizenship Programme

the founding story
In 2012, the Government of Vanuatu engaged Stanford Knight’s senior advisory team to help develop a citizenship by investment programme for the nation. Working directly with the Prime Minister’s office and the Citizenship Commission, our team contributed to the legislative framework that established the programme.

This was not a consulting engagement at arm’s length. Our team was present in Port Vila, working alongside government officials, contributing to policy design, due diligence frameworks, and operational structures.

The programme that exists today — the one that has issued thousands of citizenships and generated significant national revenue — is built on a foundation we helped create.

The designation

What “Government-Appointed Master Agent” Means

A Master Agent is not simply a licensed operator. It is a government-designated entity with specific responsibilities, access, and accountability.

It means
  • Formal recognition by the Government of Vanuatu as an authorised programme partner
  • Direct communication channels with the Citizenship Commission
  • Government remuneration (not client-funded) — SKP is paid by the Vanuatu government
  • Enhanced due diligence responsibilities — we are accountable for application quality
  • Ongoing participation in programme development and policy discussions
It does not mean
  • Guaranteed approval for every application (due diligence still applies)
  • Ability to bypass legal requirements (we operate within the legislation we helped write)
  • Exclusive government access (other agents operate legally, though without our history)

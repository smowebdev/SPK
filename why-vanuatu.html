Why Vanuatu?

Most citizenship programmes trade speed for legitimacy. Vanuatu's doesn't.

A sovereign Pacific nation — and one of the world's most established citizenship programmes.

Vanuatu is an independent republic in the South Pacific — a UN member state, a Commonwealth nation, and a functioning parliamentary democracy with a common-law legal system. English, French, and Bislama are its official languages.

Its capital, Port Vila, is a modern Pacific city with international banking infrastructure, direct flights to Australia and New Zealand, and a growing professional services sector.

Financial Benefits

A Tax-Efficient Jurisdiction

Vanuatu levies:

  • No personal income tax
  • No capital gains tax
  • No inheritance tax
  • No wealth tax
  • No withholding tax on foreign income
  • No cryptocurrency taxation

For individuals managing wealth across multiple jurisdictions, Vanuatu citizenship provides a legitimate, government-sanctioned tax-efficient base. Vanuatu’s tax framework is not a loophole — it is the country’s longstanding fiscal policy, internationally recognised.

Note: your home jurisdiction’s tax obligations continue to apply. Consult your tax adviser regarding your specific circumstances.

Offshore & Banking

International Banking & Business Structuring

Vanuatu’s financial centre offers legitimate international banking relationships, corporate structuring, and asset protection. The jurisdiction is regulated, compliant with international AML/CFT standards, and increasingly attractive to global businesses seeking Pacific-based operations.
For cryptocurrency holders and digital asset entrepreneurs, Vanuatu provides a progressive regulatory environment with no digital asset taxation.

Hereditary Citizenship

A Multigenerational Asset

Vanuatu citizenship is hereditary. Once acquired, it passes automatically to your children — and their children after them. Unlike visas, residency permits, or temporary status, citizenship is permanent and inheritable.
This makes Vanuatu citizenship not just a personal asset, but a family legacy. Your investment today creates options for generations to come.

The Legal Framework

Government-Backed. Legislated. Internationally Recognised.

Vanuatu’s Citizenship by Investment Programme operates under formal legislation passed by the national parliament. It is administered by the Citizenship Commission of Vanuatu — a government body with oversight responsibility for all applications, due diligence, and approvals.

Stanford Knight & Partners helped create this legislation in 2012 and continues to work alongside the Citizenship Commission as a government-appointed Master Agent.

The programme is not an administrative workaround or temporary policy. It is enshrined in law, operated by government, and internationally recognised.

quality of life

Life in Vanuatu

While citizenship carries no residency requirement, those who choose to spend time in Vanuatu discover a remarkable quality of life. Warm tropical climate, pristine natural environment, a welcoming local culture, and a growing expatriate community make Port Vila and the outer islands increasingly popular with global citizens.

International schools, modern healthcare, reliable telecommunications, and direct flights to Australia, New Zealand, and Fiji make Vanuatu accessible and liveable.
